## 1. Top Recurring Objections & Suggested Responses

### Objection #1: Scope & Pricing Alignment Before Commitment
**Evidence:** The Exact Customer / Gauge Capital thread shows a multi-step negotiation where Sam Yang (Gauge Capital) requested a 5–10 min pricing call *after* scope alignment, and provided framing feedback on the proposal before it went to the final decision-makers (Gary and Matt G). The Catalyst Property Company thread shows Martin Thaggard explicitly calling out appreciation for Metacto's approach of *"first scoping and pricing the planned work at its expected market value, and then separately..."* — suggesting this is a recurring friction point that Metacto has already begun addressing.

**Pattern:** Prospects want to understand what they're paying for before they commit. Proposals that bundle scope + price without a prior alignment conversation create friction.

**Suggested Response:**
> *"We always recommend a brief scope alignment call before we finalize pricing — that way the number you see reflects exactly what we've agreed to build, not a range. We can walk through the workstreams in 30 minutes and have a clean proposal to you within 48 hours."*

Use Catalyst's explicit positive feedback on this approach as a proof point in future conversations.

---

### Objection #2: Deal Stall / Decision-Maker Access
**Evidence:** Multiple deals are sitting at `decisionmakerboughtin` stage (Socialtyze $120K, Alliant ACE $132K, Gamma Strength Systems $20K, Andrew Golf Phase 4 $240K, PROHIT $30K) with close dates ranging from Q3–Q4 2026. The Exact Customer deal shows the champion (Sam Yang) coaching Metacto on how to frame the proposal *for* the actual decision-makers (Gary and Matt G), indicating the champion is bought in but the economic buyer hasn't been directly engaged yet.

**Pattern:** Champions are engaged but economic buyers are not yet in the room. Deals stall between champion buy-in and executive sign-off.

**Suggested Response:**
> *"We've found the fastest path to a signed agreement is a 20-minute executive briefing — not a sales call, but a focused conversation where we answer the three questions leadership always asks: what are we building, what does success look like in 90 days, and what's the risk if we don't move. Can we get 20 minutes with [decision-maker] this week?"*

---

### Objection #3: Project Rescue / Prior Vendor Failure Risk
**Evidence:** Multiple deals are explicitly framed as "rescue" engagements: *PROHIT Platform Rescue & Transition* ($30K, decision maker bought in), *I Follow Inc – Social Media App (Project Rescue / MVP Build)* ($90K, closed lost), *Venue Music – Project Rescue Assessment* ($5K, closed lost), *MSP Process – AI-First Engineering Takeover* ($44K, closed lost). The pattern of rescue deals going to closed-lost suggests prospects may be skeptical that a new vendor can succeed where others failed, or that the rescue scope/cost is hard to justify.

**Pattern:** Rescue engagements attract interest but close at a lower rate. Prospects are burned and cautious.

**Suggested Response:**
> *"We've done this before — and the first thing we do is an honest audit, not a sales pitch. We'll tell you exactly what's salvageable, what needs to be rebuilt, and what it will cost. If the numbers don't make sense, we'll tell you that too. Our goal is to give you a clear picture in [X days], not to inherit a problem we can't solve."*

Consider offering a fixed-fee rescue assessment as a low-risk entry point (Diamond Pulse's $7,500 Product Strategy & Technical Assessment is a model for this).
